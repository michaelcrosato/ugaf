## Executive summary

The core fantasy — read the Hush, deduce its lawful physics, exploit them to carry the core out — is genuinely strong and the prose is the demo's standout asset, but the slice as shipped is a tight ~10-minute clever short story rather than the "10 min → 2 hr → 25 replay" dwell engine the spec promises, because **every advertised replay/depth system is currently configured into its no-op position at exactly the seam where it should bite.** The single most-corroborated finding (5 of 6 dimensions independently) is that the headline Greywater night-law never fires: the clock as tuned lets every winning run reach and rob the core during day/dusk, so the most-telegraphed threat costs nobody anything, deduction is rewardless and indistinguishable from buying victory, and three "winning" personas converge on one byte-identical route and one byte-identical epilogue. The good news from round 1's pattern holds: nearly every fix below is a data/config change against an architecture that already supports the intended behavior — the laws-as-data, `liveLaws`/`startKits`/`rerollRumorTruth` plumbing, drift hooks, and `look.variants` predicate system are all present and correct; they are simply turned off.

**Strongest cross-dimension signals (do these first):** Greywater never bites (5 dims), all solution paths + the epilogue collapse to one rote outcome (4 dims: fun/balance/replay), and the law codex/inventory remain unreachable through the canonical PROCTOR path even after round 1 (3 dims: fairness/parser/replay — still open, verified).

## Top fixes (do these)

### 1. Make the Greywater night-law actually fire on the optimal path — P0 — balance
**Corroborated by 5 of 6 dimensions (fun, fairness, prose, balance, replay).** The most-advertised lethal law (3 NPCs, the waystation map, a dead-adventurer tell) never triggers in any winning run. Day starts ~16:08 (`assemble.ts` default `startMinutes = 16*60`) and night falls ~20:45; the straight-line route `out→road→road→on→fork→water→in→cache→take core→back` costs only ~3.5–4.5 game-hours, so a player heading straight in always reaches the core and leaves during dusk. The night variants authored in `world.ts` ("Any worked metal you carry is dying in your hands") and the `degrade_item_class` effect (`laws.ts:42`) are dead content. The knowledge-as-progression pillar is gutted at its centerpiece — you win by walking fast, not by knowing anything.
**Fix:** Re-time the funnel so a blind player's *first* trip to the cache lands at the water near nightfall. Concretely, any/all of: (a) push start time later via `GameOptions.startMinutes` (arrive ~19:30 so dusk→night lands while still deliberating in town); (b) raise the distance on the three wading legs (`e_fork_grey`, `e_grey_bottoms`, `e_bottoms_cache`); (c) gate the pump-house cache so it only opens after dark for a diegetic reason ("the shelf is submerged until the bottoms drop at night"). Goal: convert "go now, by daylight" from a strict dominator into one valid solution among three, so "go now and risk iron" vs "strip metal / wait for day / buy the safe hour" becomes a real, felt choice. Add a regression line asserting a transcript-visible Greywater first-contact tell on a straight run.

### 2. Wire codex / inventory / recall through the canonical (PROCTOR) path — P0 — system
**Corroborated by 3 dimensions (fairness, parser, replay). Verified STILL OPEN after round 1.** `Session.codex()`/`inventory()` exist (`src/game/session.ts:143, 167`) but are dispatched ONLY in the readline CLI (`src/cli/main.ts:83–90`). The PROCTOR path — which `protocol.ts` declares the stable interface — runs `session.parse() → session.applyIntent()` (`protocol.ts:124–125`) and never intercepts these, so `codex`/`inventory` fall through to `(nothing happens)`; `recall` routes to spine and no-ops. A real MCP player cannot open the fallible codex — the literal scoreboard of the central pillar — cannot see which laws they've surveyed, and cannot review rumor reliability before acting on a lethal false rumor.
**Fix:** In `Session.applyIntent` (so both CLI and PROCTOR inherit it), intercept meta intents BEFORE kernel routing and return `{text, status:'active'}` WITHOUT committing a turn: `recall`/bare `codex`/`laws`/`journal` → `this.codex()`; `inventory`/`i`/`inv` → `this.inventory()`; `map` → exits; `help`/`?` → HELP. Add these as `narrator/parse.ts` synonyms. Add a regression assert in the playthrough test that `recall` returns the codex text under the PROCTOR path so this can't silently regress.

### 3. Make deduction legibly *pay*, and buying *cost* — P0 — balance/content
**Corroborated by 3 dimensions (fun, balance, replay).** `curious-methodical` deduces both laws; `social-buyer` deduces NOTHING, gets the Greywater secret free from Mox dialogue, walks in — and reaches the **byte-identical** "✦ The Core, Carried Out" epilogue. `insight` is decremented but never visibly spent on anything the player wants; a reward you can't perceive doesn't exist.
**Fix:** Make a *surveyed* law do something a rumor/bought-map can't, and have bought knowledge enter the codex as `referenced (purchased — unverified)` rather than `surveyed`, so the buyer still risks a wrong/drifted rumor and must verify in the field. Concretely: (1) only a surveyed Mile Road unlocks a safe fast-travel that doesn't accrue distance; (2) reaching the core with all three laws surveyed unlocks a distinct epilogue paragraph vs a "bought passage" variant; (3) optionally let surveyed laws be sold back to Eun for coin, giving the deducer an economy the buyer lacks. Pairs with fix #4 (epilogue must branch) and #1 (without a real night threat, neither path's knowledge is ever needed).

### 4. Add at least one persistent, choice-keyed consequence to the epilogue — P0 — system
**Corroborated by 2 dimensions (fun, replay).** Nothing in `world.*` mutates from player choice; `reputation` is in `PLAYER_VISIBLE_NS` (`assemble.ts:28`) and declared writable by social but never written. All three winning runs reach the same end-state, making the four personas mechanically interchangeable once they reach the cache and gutting the immersive-sim "consequence as memory" replay driver.
**Fix:** Wire one choice-keyed world-fact into the ending. Minimum viable: set `reputation.survey` / `reputation.striders` when the player buys a law-map vs a route, and `world.holdout.supply` on take-vs-leave, then add 2–3 conditional epilogue variants (the `look.variants` / `when` predicate system already works in `world.ts`). E.g. "The Survey will want their core back" if `reputation.striders>0` vs "Eun files your observations and says nothing about the empty shelf." (The bribe path that sets these facts is already unblocked as of round 1.)

### 5. Turn on per-seed law variance — every seed currently runs all three laws — P0 — content
**Single-dimension (replay) but structural, and the cheapest high-leverage replay lever.** `content/hush/index.ts:54`: `liveLaws: { min:3, max:3, always:['mile_road','greywater','antenna_field'] }`. The `liveLaws()` shuffle (`assemble.ts:63–81`) is fully implemented but, with `min=max=3` and all three in `always`, per-seed which-laws variance is structurally impossible. A second run is the same world; replay collapses to "optimal vs reckless," which the transcripts already prove is a binary.
**Fix:** Author a 4th/5th anchor law (e.g. a causality law "the ford repeats your last move," a perception law "the antennas hide an exit until you stop speaking"), keep `always:['mile_road','greywater']` (the teaching laws), set `liveLaws { min:3, max:4 }` drawing the rotating slot from the new pool. ~Half of seeds then surface a never-seen law — zero engine cost, the shuffle already exists.

### 6. Tune Law Drift to the slice's actual length — it never fires — P0 — balance
**Single-dimension (replay) but it's the named mechanism for "keeping mastery fun."** Drift windows are 40/50/60 turns (`laws.ts:32,55,80`) and `applyDrift()` only acts when `turn % everyTurns === 0` AND the law is `surveyed`; the longest run wins at ~26 turns, so the gate is never satisfied and no drift/decay line appears in any transcript. The one system designed for long-session dwell and the "betrayal vs delight" loop is dead code.
**Fix:** Either shorten windows to ~14/18/22, OR (cleaner) gate drift on *dwell-turns-since-survey* (e.g. `driftAfter: 8` surveyed turns) so it fires relative to when mastery was achieved rather than absolute turn count. Add one transcript-visible drift tell to the acceptance test.

### 7. Give the second Changed strike a one-turn, signposted escape window — P1 — balance
**Corroborated by 2 dimensions (fairness, fun).** Warn-before-kill holds in the letter: first `say maren` is a non-lethal first-contact ("Be quiet now, or be gone"), the second kills on the immediately following turn. But there is no cooldown, no persistent "the Changed is still near" state, and no flee affordance in the warning, so the fairness margin is one turn of perfect play — a twitch test, not a knowledge test — and the name-stones tell can be skipped entirely so lethality is never taught before it's tested.
**Fix:** On first contact set a transient `law.antenna_field.hunted=true` and render a persistent room-state tell next look/ambient ("The Changed is still out there, circling the hum. Get clear of the field, or be silent."). Make the second strike lethal only if the player re-triggers (speaks) OR remains in the field ≥1 more turn; leaving clears the state non-lethally. Auto-trigger the name-stones dead-adventurer glimpse on entering the field at night so the law is taught before it's tested.

### 8. Make rumor reliability disconfirmable in-world, and surface conflicts — P1 — law/content
**Corroborated by 2 dimensions (fairness, replay).** The false rumors (Lyle's "the bottoms eat gold," "say your OWN name there… the field knows you as kin") are heard and stored, but the only reliability signal lives in the codex (unreachable until fix #2), and the antenna lie is self-disconfirming only fatally. A social-buyer who trusts Lyle over Holt has no fair path to catch the contradiction except by nearly dying. Separately, `rerollRumorTruth:false` (`index.ts:55`) means the same NPC tells the same lie every seed, so once a returning player knows Lyle lies, the buyer path is solved forever.
**Fix:** (a) When a second rumor on the same topic with opposite truth is acquired, surface a one-line codex/ambient note: "Two people have told you opposite things about the antennas. One of them is wrong, and wrong here is fatal." (b) Add a non-lethal disconfirmer for the gold rumor at the ford (examine silt: worked iron slumps, gold untouched). (c) Set `rerollRumorTruth:true` and author each rumor as a truth/distortion pair keyed to the law, so "who to trust" is a fresh deduction each seed (pairs with fix #5).

### 9. Make unclassified input a free, helpful nudge — never a silent turn-burn — P1 — parser
**Corroborated by 2 dimensions (parser, fairness).** Unknown input (`xyzzy`, `sniff`) returns `(nothing happens)` AND silently commits a beat that advances the clock — and since dusk→night gates the lethal Greywater law (once fix #1 lands), a confused player typing nonsense is quietly pushed toward nightfall with zero feedback. The spine's "surfaced as a parser nudge instead" comment describes a nudge that doesn't exist (`narrator/parse.ts:153` returns `unclassified` and the turn still commits).
**Fix:** When `intent.class === 'unclassified'`, do NOT commit a turn or advance the clock; return a polite parser message naming what IS understood and echo the unrecognized word for OOPS-style correction: "You're not sure how to 'xyzzy' here. Try LOOK, GO <way>, EXAMINE <thing>, LISTEN, or TALK TO <someone>. (CODEX shows what you've learned; MAP shows the ways out.)"

### 10. Return authored NPC prose on examine; broaden `deduce`/`examine` failure handling — P1 — parser/content
**Corroborated by 2 dimensions (parser, fairness).** Two related guess-the-verb seams:
- `look at the warden` → generic "nothing here tells you anything you didn't already know," discarding Holt's hand-written `look.base` (`npcs.ts:26`). `describeExamine` consults examinables/tellLibrary but never `npc.look`. **Fix:** in `describeExamine`, if the target resolves to a present NPC, return `npc.look` before falling through to tells. All four NPCs already have authored look blocks.
- `deduce` dead-ends when the topic is a place-noun ("deduce the road," "deduce the antennas") rather than the exact law title, returning "not even sure what rule you are reaching for." `matchLaw` only matches law id/title substrings. **Fix:** broaden `matchLaw` to also match each law's scope node ids/region and seen examinable nouns; when exactly one law is in scope with ≥1 tell observed, name the candidate ("Do you mean the law of the Mile Road? Try: deduce the mile road.") instead of the bare dead-end.

### 11. Make dropped items recoverable, and accept named destinations as go-targets — P1 — parser/system
**Single-dimension (parser) but two reversibility/legibility defects, each cheap:**
- **Dropped items vanish:** `drop the lantern` succeeds, then `take the lantern` → "nothing like that here to take" (a lie — it's at the player's feet). `travel` drop deletes possession facts but never registers the item at the node; `take.validateLegality` only checks the static `nodeItems` map. This breaks "a botched approach always leaves a path back," and dropping metal to dodge the Greywater is a plausibly *law-relevant* choice. **Fix:** on drop, push the item into a runtime `droppedHere[node]` list (or a fact); have `take.validateLegality` and the scene renderer consult it.
- **Named destinations rejected:** `go to the holdout` → "no way like that," though the prose names "the Holdout" (`world.ts:57`). `matchExit` only matches `e.dir`/`e.to`/`e.label`. **Fix:** also match the *target node's* title/region in `matchExit`; add a few hand aliases (`holdout`/`rest`/`lyle` → the road exit).

### 12. Author bespoke prose for the demo's two most important moments: the prize, and the dark — P1 — prose/content
**Corroborated by 2 dimensions (prose, balance).** Two authored-but-invisible quality leaks (note: the "You take core" article bug itself was already fixed in round 1; the remaining gap is *bespoke flavor*, not grammar):
- **The prize is generic.** Even with articles, the take-result/presence-line for the core is plain engine-speak against a high-literary room ("warm and wrongly heavy"). Author a bespoke beat: take → "You close your hand around the core. It is warmer than it should be, and it settles into your pack like it has decided to come."
- **The strongest night prose never fires** because every player reaches the bottoms at dusk (fix #1 is the structural cause). Until #1 lands, add a dusk-tier variant to `greywater_bottoms`/`ford` so even the daylight-rush gets a tightening tell ("the hum is starting to find the iron in your kit — you have minutes, not hours"). Also author 2–3 per-region nightfall variants so the single repeated "Dusk settles over the Hush" stinger stops reading as a metronome, and make ambient selection a no-repeat rotation per node (high-traffic hubs currently re-serve the identical "mending a net" line every visit).

## What is already working (keep; do not regress)

- **Round 1's fixes hold and should not be reopened:** warn-before-kill, the unblocked bribe/`objective` write, the exit-word-vs-verb precedence, return-words, and the core article nits are all verified in current code. The four critic transcripts predate them.
- **The prose voice at its best is exactly the "wrong-but-lawful, eerie, indifferent" register the brief asks for** — the dishonest mileposts, the dead walker churning "a thousand looping bootprints," the horizon "the colour of a held breath," the Greywater "calling iron home to the mud," and both epilogues. All 6 dimensions volunteered praise here.
- **The Antenna first-contact beat ("It brushes past you, cold as well-water… You are unhurt. You will not be, twice.") is the single best dread moment in the corpus** — proof the warn-before-kill + fail-safe pattern works when it fires. The work is getting the other laws' equivalent beats to fire too.
- **Warn-before-kill is mechanically real and demonstrable; the two reversible laws cannot kill at all.** No transcript shows an unforeseeable instakill — the fairness bones are sound.
- **The architecture for high variance and depth is present and correct:** laws-as-data, deterministic effect-categories, `liveLaws`/`startKits`/`rerollRumorTruth` plumbing in `assemble.ts`, drift hooks, the `look.variants`/`when` predicate system (the carrying-the-core waystation variant fires nicely), and full determinism/replay/realness verification. Almost every Top fix is config/data, not engine work.
- **The verb lexicon is already generous** — bare directions, RETURN_WORDS, longest-match verb phrases, verb-aware noun preference, retrace-on-back. The parser groundwork toward "Polite or better" is solid; fixes #9–#11 close the remaining seams.

## Deferred / nice-to-have

- **Give at least one law an offensive/utility "wield" verb** so the set flips from a hazard checklist to a toybox (weaponize the antenna law to lure the Changed onto a pursuer; deliberately look-back to strand a pursuer on the lengthening Mile Road). High replay upside, more design than the Top-12 config fixes. _(fun, P1)_
- **Add a second-order activity layer** so the slice isn't one errand down a 14-node corridor and back: a second sellable salvage so coins finally matter, a live return trip where carrying the core provokes a faction interception (the `awareness.cordon_patrol`/`flag.hidden` stealth path is already modeled), a node whose state changes on the way back. _(fun, P1)_
- **Make start-kits cross-cut multiple laws/economy** instead of only swapping which metal you carry (`broke`/`pry` are currently mechanically identical; `light` trivially pre-solves the Greywater). Give `light` more coin (nudges the buy path), make `pry` a bigger Greywater liability that opens a shortcut, add a `mapped` kit starting one law at `referenced`. _(replay, P1)_
- **Open the Antenna Field as a genuine alternate corridor to the core** (a path that bypasses the ford but demands the antenna law be solved) rather than a death-spur the optimal player skips, and unblock the wire-gap stealth route trading Cordon-patrol consequence for speed. _(replay, P1)_
- **Give the Mile Road observable bite** — `look back` is currently consequence-free beyond "Unsettled 1"; the doubled-distance-behind never forces a longer return leg in any transcript. Make the next return leg cost ~double game-time and say so; tie Unsettled stacks to a visible threshold / soft fail. _(balance, P1)_
- **Decide whether Exposure/Unsettled should bite this slice** — surfaced as HUD counters with no shown consequence. Either wire a visible threshold ("Unsettled 3 = the road takes you") or suppress the counters so they don't imply unmet stakes. Tying exposure to time-in-Zone-after-dark would reinforce the night-Greywater dilemma. _(balance, P2)_
- **De-duplicate the dead-walker examine** (the `look.base` image and the tell cue print near-identical text back-to-back) and add one learned-law prose variant per anomaly region so knowledge shows in description, not just the codex. _(prose, P2)_
- **Reskin generic refusal prose in-voice** ("The Hush does not offer you that."; route `sniff`/`smell` to the region smell palette instead of burning a turn) and steer bare `examine`/`take` failures to the room's advertised affordances. _(prose/parser, P2)_
- **Tighten the Greywater's lethal *framing* to match its reversible *mechanics*** — rumors/lore teach a deadlier law (knives → rust-mud "when they needed them") than the metal-only degrade that exists; chain metal-loss → vulnerability in the dead-walker prose so the law reads consistently with its foreshadowing. _(fairness, P2)_
- **Surface the deduce/codex/look-back verbs diegetically** (a one-time footer hint once a law has ≥`minTellsToSurvey` tells observed; an Eun/Survey index-card introduction) so probing players discover the core verbs without the CLI banner the harness never prints. Depends on fix #2. _(fun/fairness, P2)_

# feedback/0004 — Loop B round 3 synthesis (the IMPROVED Hush)

> 22 findings, 4 critics (new_systems, fun_dwell, fairness_clarity, prose_greatness). Round 2
> fixed the FRAMING; round 3 critics agree it did not fix the STAKES. This file merges duplicates,
> flags cross-dimension agreement, and ranks the ~8 highest-leverage fixes.

## Executive summary

No — it is not great yet, but the gap is now narrow, concrete, and unusually concentrated: the
Hush is a polished ~10-15 minute clever short story with three honest paths, distinct earned
epilogues, and a top-tier prose voice, but it is still not the 2-hour / 25-replay dwell engine the
brief demands. The single root cause, named independently by all four critics, is that **the
player's central choice writes facts that nothing in the game reads** — the Greywater degrade sets
`item.condition='ore'`, the laws accrue `exposure`/`unsettled`/`closer`, and the return trip and
reputation debt exist, yet no exit gates on them, no threshold consumes them, and no prose varies by
them, so a slumped knife plays identically to a good one and a master sees identical scenery to a
tourist. The distance to "greatest in genre" is roughly one structural feature (a costed return-trip
interception that makes the metal/debt decision bite on the way out) plus three near-free prose
hooks (knowledge-keyed room variants, escalating-not-repeating dread, guaranteed first-contact
beats) — the engine already supports all of it; the content does not yet use it.

## Top fixes (do these)

1. **Make the metal/return decision actually cost something — a costed return-trip interception.**
   - Severity: **P0** · Area: content/balance
   - Corroboration: **3 critics / 4 findings** (fun_dwell ×2 — "consequence-free degrade" + "no return-trip interception"; fairness_clarity — "headline warning costs nothing"). Strongest cross-dimension agreement in the set.
   - Problem: The most-telegraphed law (3 NPCs + waystation notice + 2 corpse tells) writes `item.condition='ore'`, but `Grep` confirms the only reader is `session.ts:205` (inventory display). No exit gates on it, `take core` needs no tool, the goal checks only possession + surveyed laws. So "carry iron in / strip it / buy the hour" is a fake choice — all three reach the identical core-grab, and the whole second half of the loop (the walk home) is consequence-free filler. The stealth FSM, `awareness.cordon_patrol`, `flag.hidden`, the guarded `e_check_fork` shortcut, and `reputation.pc.striders` are fully modeled but fire in **zero** transcripts.
   - Concrete fix: On `take core`, set a Strider tail / cordon patrol to `alert` and schedule an interception at the fork/checkpoint on the return. Resolve it by **reading a now-live system**: a working iron tool widens the guarded `e_check_fork` wire-gap (a slumped `ore` one fails — "the bar folds like warm wax"); OR hide via the stealth route (already opens when `awareness!=alert && flag.hidden`); OR surrender coin/standing (the Mox-debt path, `reputation.pc.striders>=1`). This single feature activates **five dead systems at once** (`.condition`, stealth, awareness/`flag.hidden`, the guarded shortcut, reputation) and converts the bought-vs-read distinction from epilogue flavour into felt gameplay.

2. **A blind player cannot discover DEDUCE — the verb the mastery win requires.**
   - Severity: **P0** · Area: ux
   - Corroboration: 1 critic (fairness_clarity) but flagged as that critic's **single strongest item** — it gates the headline epilogue. No cross-dim conflict; orthogonal to the others.
   - Problem: `recover_core_mastery` requires `known.law.*=='surveyed'`, only reachable via the `deduce`/`survey` intent, but DEDUCE is surfaced **nowhere** a PROCTOR/blind player sees: the footer says only "You could look closer at…" (EXAMINE), the NOT_UNDERSTOOD nudge lists LOOK/GO/EXAMINE/LISTEN/SEARCH/TALK TO and omits DEDUCE, the codex shows "approximate" with no hint that deduction confirms it. A fresh player can fill the codex to "approximate" and be structurally unable to reach "Read True."
   - Concrete fix: (a) add `DEDUCE <law>` to the NOT_UNDERSTOOD verb list (`session.ts:90`); (b) once a law hits "approximate", emit a one-time diegetic footer hint ("You have seen enough to almost name the rule. Try: DEDUCE THE MILE ROAD.") gated on a once-per-law flag; (c) echo the same nudge in the codex line for any "approximate" law.

3. **Gate the Hollow Dark's tells/deduction on `law.live`, and pin the showcase seed.**
   - Severity: **P0** · Area: parser/content
   - Corroboration: **2 critics / 3 findings** (new_systems ×2 — "showcase seed cordon-7 has it not live" + "non-live law fully learnable"; fairness_clarity — "false warning ~half the time"). Cross-dimension agreement.
   - Problem: `invest/index.ts` never reads `law.live`; the Fork's sitter examinable and `hollow_silence` listen text are authored unconditionally, and deduce surveys on `seen>=minTells` with no live check. On ~half of seeds the Hollow Dark is dead, yet the Fork still emits a lethal-sounding warning and the player can `deduce the hollow dark` to "surveyed" — certifying a rule the world never enforces. Worse, the dawdler showcase (`scripts/playtest.ts:59`, seed `cordon-7`) runs on a seed where it is **not live**, so the artifact meant to prove law-fairness shows a non-law with no enforcement.
   - Concrete fix: Gate the Hollow Dark's tells/examinables/ambient on `law.hollow_dark.live` (a `when:{fact:'law.hollow_dark.live',eq:true}` variant with a neutral fallback), and skip live-gated deduction. Pin the dawdler showcase to a live seed (`seed-0`) and assert `contacts>=1`. Then a dead seed shows a quiet ordinary Fork; a live seed shows the threat — a real present/absent variance with no false warning.

4. **Author knowledge-keyed room variants — make mastery visible in the world, not just the codex.**
   - Severity: **P1** · Area: prose
   - Corroboration: 1 critic (prose_greatness) as its **central miss** (touches 5 of 6 craft pillars), and it is the prose-side mirror of fix #1's "facts nobody reads" — strong thematic agreement across dims even though only one critic filed it.
   - Problem: `content/hush/world.ts` has exactly ONE law-keyed variant (`law.antenna_field.active`) and ZERO knowledge-keyed ones. The deducer who surveys the Mile Road and Greywater sees every node render **byte-identical** to a tourist's; comprehension appears only in the codex. The plumbing exists — `invest` commits `known.law.${id}`, `render.ts compose()` runs every variant's `when` through `evalPredicate` — the prose just doesn't use it. The most literary payoff in the genre (the uncanny resolving into legible law before your eyes) is left on the table.
   - Concrete fix: Add one `{ when:{ fact:'known.law.<id>', eq:'surveyed' }, text:'…' }` variant per anchor law, ~1 sentence each, appended post-survey (e.g. Greywater: "You read the water now the way you read a clock… It is just a law, and you know its shape."). Zero engine work.

5. **Wire ONE real threshold for exposure/unsettled — or suppress the counters.**
   - Severity: **P1** · Area: balance
   - Corroboration: **2 critics / 2 findings** (fun_dwell + fairness_clarity, near-verbatim agreement). Both confirm via `Grep` that nothing reads `survival.pc.exposure`/`unsettled` for a threshold — they are written and displayed only.
   - Problem: The HUD shows "exposure 2" / "Unsettled 1" climbing toward a consequence that never arrives. An attentive player (and both careful personas) watches a meter rise expecting it to matter — a broken promise that erodes the "everything-has-a-rule" trust the game is built on.
   - Concrete fix: Wire one visible threshold each and announce it the turn before it bites — e.g. `unsettled>=3` on the Mile Road can dump you back a node ("the road takes you"), and exposure as a night-in-Zone clock (`>=5` drops patrol detection threshold / closes the Hollow Dark one step faster). Cheaper honest fallback if out of scope: **suppress the counters until they bite** so they stop advertising unmet danger.

6. **Give the Hollow Dark teeth (or soften its prose) — and verify wait/rest first-contact actually fires.**
   - Severity: **P1** · Area: law
   - Corroboration: 1 critic (fairness_clarity) but couples directly with #5 (exposure clock) and #3 (live-gating). The "warn-before-kill inverted" framing is the trust risk both fairness findings share.
   - Problem: The tells teach a lethal escalating law ("to wait or rest is FATAL patience"; "the dark closes the distance"), but the effect is `impose_condition unsettled severity:'reversible'` — it can never kill, and the `closer` counter it writes is read by nothing. In `dawdler.md` the player `rest`/`wait` in live Hollow Dark scope at night and **no first-contact warning fired and unsettled never accrued**. Surveying "fatal patience," waiting, and taking zero harm teaches the player the codex over-warns — which poisons trust in the codex's REAL lethal warnings (the Antenna).
   - Concrete fix: Either (a) read `law.hollow_dark.closer` and at `>=3` in scope at night escalate to a **warned** soft-fail (a forced flight + exposure, with a clear pre-threshold tell), pairing with #5; OR (b) if it must stay reversible, reframe the tell prose from death to nerve-loss. Either way, fix the wait/rest → `law_trigger` path (the dawdler evidence shows it isn't reaching the intent) and add a regression asserting a Hollow-Dark first-contact tell on a night rest in scope.

7. **Make dusk/night dread ESCALATE on revisit instead of re-printing verbatim; guarantee first-contact beats fire.**
   - Severity: **P1 / P2** · Area: prose
   - Corroboration: 2 prose findings merged ("static verbatim dread block" P1 + "best lethal first-contact prose is dead content" P2). Single-critic (prose) but two distinct evidence chains.
   - Problem: The ~50-word "you have minutes, not hours" Greywater block re-prints **verbatim** at 19:26 inbound and again at 20:42 outbound — `render.ts compose()` rotates `ambient` deterministically but emits `variants` text unchanged. Escalation reads as a metronome; the prose peaks on first contact then flatlines. Separately, the corpus's **best lethal prose** — the Greywater "folds like wet clay" and Hollow Dark "the dark leans in" first-contact beats — fires in **zero** transcripts (only the Antenna first-contact actually fires, and it is the standout precisely because it does).
   - Concrete fix: Add a terser, more-urgent second-tier dusk variant gated on re-entry with the threat advanced (`known.tell.grey_rust_bloom` OR `possession.pc.salvage_core`), with `replace:true` so it supplants the long block. And render the Greywater/Hollow-Dark first-contact tell as an **event line** the first turn `degrade_item_class` / a night rest actually bites — distinct from the room re-description.

8. **Add discoverable depth so the side-rooms and a second run pay off (second salvage + drift that matters).**
   - Severity: **P1 / P2** · Area: content/balance
   - Corroboration: 3 fun_dwell findings merged ("single-errand corridor" P1, "drift fires in zero transcripts" P1, "rotating 4th law only via dawdling" P2) + the new_systems overall. This is the bundle that most directly addresses the "not a 2-hour engine" verdict.
   - Problem: The map is a 14-node line; the Survey camp, Striders camp, and entire Antenna Field are optional spurs a straight run never enters, and the Antenna is all risk / no reward. Law Drift — the named "keep mastery fun" system — fires in zero transcripts because `driftAfter` (14/16/12) is tuned longer than a focused win (~26 turns). The rotating Hollow Dark only manifests if the player deliberately dawdles in the dark, an anti-pattern the game otherwise punishes — so a normal second run feels identical to the first.
   - Concrete fix: (a) Add a **second sellable salvage** off the optimal line (e.g. an anomalous relic in the Antenna Field reachable only by solving the silence-discipline law) that Eun/Mox buy — turning the death-spur into an optional high-skill score run and giving coins a use. (b) Shorten Greywater/Antenna/Hollow-Dark `driftAfter` to ~8-10 (or make drift mutate a law's **parameters** visibly so re-surveying yields a genuinely different rule), with a regression asserting a Greywater `drift_demote` is reachable in a long exploratory run. (c) Let the return-interception (#1) push goal-directed players into the deep after dark so they meet the Hollow Dark organically.

## What is now excellent (do not touch)

- **The prose voice at its best** — "wrong-but-lawful, indifferent" lands repeatedly (the dead walker's "thousand looping bootprints," the horizon "the colour of a held breath," the Greywater "calling iron home to the mud," the Antenna "cold as well-water… You will not be, twice"). All four critics call the voice the corpus standout.
- **Fairness of DEATHS** — every lethal law now warns before it bites with a real escape: the Antenna gives non-lethal first contact + a clean leave-the-field escape (lethal only on the SECOND name), the Greywater bites at dusk with the "minutes, not hours" tell. fairness_clarity: "Round 3 has genuinely closed the big fairness gaps… none of these are unfair deaths."
- **Branching, earned epilogues** — "Read True" (mastery) vs "on a Bought Map" vs nerve-not-knowing read as distinct, earned moral conclusions; the Striders remember the debt.
- **Reachable codex/inventory/map via the PROCTOR path**, and the forgiving "Polite-or-better" parser with free helpful nudges.

### Honest verdict on "greatest in genre"

The Hush is **one structural feature and three near-free prose hooks away** from credibly competing
at the top of the genre, and the architecture already supports every fix with zero or near-zero
engine work. The remaining distance is not breadth (the systems exist) but **wiring**: round 3's
deepest failure mode is *written-but-unread state* — `condition`, `closer`, `exposure`, `unsettled`,
reputation debt, and knowledge stage are all committed and none are consumed by a gate, a threshold,
or a variant. Fix #1 (consume the metal/debt facts on the return leg) and fix #4 (consume the
knowledge facts in the prose) attack the same root from the systems and prose sides respectively;
together they convert ~5 dead systems into live stakes and make mastery legible in the fiction. Do
those two and the dwell-engine + greatness gap closes most of the way in a single round. Until then
it is an excellent short story with a great voice that does not yet reward a second hour or a second
run.

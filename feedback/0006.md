# Feedback 0006 — The Verdict Round

## Verdict

The Hush is now genuinely close to competing at the top of its genre — the core loop, the warn-before-kill fairness, and the prose voice are all already at or near greatest-in-class, and on the two completed transcripts the read-exploit-slip-home fantasy lands. But it is held back from the top by exactly two open wounds, both small and both fixable: a single balance bug that makes the flagship "Read True" mastery ending *unreachable on the very run that earns it*, and a single render-plumbing leak that makes the build's best escalation beat (the carry-the-core Greywater re-entry) *stack three contradictory urgency paragraphs* instead of replacing them. Fix those two and this is a credible top-of-genre demo; until then the headline reward is dead content and the highest-craft prose moment flattens, so the distance remaining is short but it sits squarely on the two things the brief is built around.

## Top remaining fixes (only the genuinely-open, high-leverage items)

1. **P0 — Make the mastery "Read True" ending reachable (balance).** The goal `recover_core_mastery` (`content/hush/index.ts:38-50`) is a *live-state* check requiring `known.law.mile_road == 'surveyed'` AND `known.law.greywater == 'surveyed'` at the waystation, but drift (`driftAfter: 8`/`9` in `content/hush/laws.ts:32,59`) demotes both to `'approximate'` (`src/modules/anomaly/index.ts:273`) before the player gets home, so the master falls through to the generic `recover_core` ending whose text literally reads "more by nerve than by knowing" (`index.ts:72`) — handed to the one player who did it by knowing. Fix as a high-water-mark: on first reaching `surveyed`, also set a sticky `ever_surveyed` that `drift_demote` never clears, and test `ever_surveyed` in the mastery predicate. Add a regression that deduces both anchor laws, completes the there-and-back, and asserts the epilogue title contains "Read True." **This is the single highest-leverage fix in the build.**

2. **P1 — Give `compose()` variant precedence so `replace:true` actually supplants (render plumbing).** `src/narrator/render.ts:46-52` sets `base = v.text` on a `replace` variant but still pushes every other matching variant, so the carry-the-core Greywater block (authored `replace:true`) renders as a terse "Move." immediately followed by two slow literary paragraphs restating the same fact — the corpus's most-visible flatten, and it lands on its single highest-craft moment. When any matching variant has `replace:true`, emit only that variant for the block this turn. This one fix also closes most of the "first-contact block re-prints verbatim on re-entry" finding.

3. **P2 (cheap, do them with the above) — close the discoverability + tail leaks.** (a) Add `USE` and `GIVE` to `HELP_TEXT` and the `NOT_UNDERSTOOD` verb list in `src/game/session.ts` — these gate the two marquee round-3 systems and are the only live verbs the game never advertises. (b) Surface the relic trade in live play: its only hint is buried in `look.base` (`content/hush/world.ts:24`) and fires in zero transcripts — promote a self-advertising presence line and one Eun dialogue hook. (c) Suppress the generic "nothing you didn't already know" tail (`src/modules/invest/index.ts:151`) when authored look prose already rendered — it currently contradicts the player's first investigative act. (d) Regionalize the dusk/night transition stinger (currently one verbatim string for every region).

## What is now great

- **The core loop and the interception.** Reading the Hush, exploiting its laws, and slipping the watched gate home is genuinely compelling; the costed return-interception bites and is escapable three ways with `HIDE` as a softlock-proof universal fallback.
- **Fairness is at greatest-in-genre level.** Every lethal law warns before it kills with a real, signposted escape (Antenna non-lethal first contact, Greywater "minutes, not hours" tell that only degrades, Hollow Dark live-gated against false warns); `DEDUCE` is discoverable; parser rejections are free, helpful, option-listing nudges.
- **The prose VOICE.** "A thousand looping bootprints," the horizon "the colour of a held breath," the Greywater "calling iron home to the mud," the Antenna first-contact, the knowledge-keyed variants ("eyes front, chin level, never once turning round"), and all three authored epilogues fire and land in the transcripts. The sentences are already there; only the render plumbing holds the ceiling down.
- **The "bought map" branch.** `recover_core_bought` works, reaches its distinct ending, and reads great — proof the multi-ending architecture is sound and that fix #1 unlocks the third branch rather than inventing it.

## Recommended stopping point?

Not yet — fixes #1 and #2 are small, surgical, and target the exact two things the brief is built around (the flagship reward and the highest-craft beat), so one more focused iteration is high-value; after that lands and is regression-tested, this is a strong, shippable demo and further polish is optional.

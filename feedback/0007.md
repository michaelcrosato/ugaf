## Top fixes (ranked) — all P2

### 1. Examining an NPC prints authored prose, then contradicts it — P2 — prose/parser  ← **fixing this round**
**Visible in `wanderer.md:52-55` (a fresh transcript), and flagged still-open as `0006 #3c`.**
```
> look at the warden
  A Cordon warden with the thousand-yard look of someone who has signed too many MISSING forms. He does not want to know your name.
  You look closely, but nothing here tells you anything you didn’t already know.
```
The first line is the NPC's hand-written `look.base` (`content/hush/npcs.ts:26`), rendered by
`describeExamine` (`render.ts:117`). The second is the invest module's `invest.nothing_new` beat
(`invest/index.ts:162`): both modules claim `examine`, and invest fires its generic tail because an NPC is
not an authored *examinable*. The engine already suppresses this exact tail for examinables
(`invest/index.ts:156-160` — "stay quiet rather than contradict it") but the guard never covered NPCs. The
result directly contradicts the player on their **first investigative act**, against the game's strongest
asset — the prose voice. **Fix:** extend the existing examinable-suppression guard to also stay quiet when the
examine target is a known NPC (whose look prose `describeExamine` already supplies). One-line, surgical, in
the established pattern. (Bare targetless `examine` keeps the generic line — that case has no authored prose to
contradict.)

# feedback/0008 — the first real blind-LLM swarm (night1) + the P0 NPC/economy fix

> Source: **30 blind LLM players** driven through the new PROCTOR **MCP** harness
> (`npm run swarm`, see `docs/BLIND-SWARM.md`) — genuine blind play, not scripted tapes. 8 cynical
> high-expectations personas across mixed models (opus/sonnet/haiku); **30/30 realness-VERIFIED, 28 won,
> 0 lost**. Compiled by an opus synth pass that separated game issues from harness artifacts. This is the
> first cycle of Loop B as it was always meant to work: real players who only see the game, reporting back.

## Executive summary

The demo's skeleton is genuinely strong and the players say so unprompted: **all 30** were hooked by the
opening, **all 30** praised the prose ("sharp, not purple"), and ~13 independently named `DEDUCE` + the codex
the best parser mechanic they'd seen in years. The through-line of the critique is equally consistent: **the
front half promises lethal, reactive, information-economy gameplay the back half doesn't keep.** NPCs are
one-line vending machines that don't notice you're carrying the thing the whole map hunts; the advertised
knowledge-economy doesn't transact; and the lethal stakes never actually bite (28/30 won, many "never
threatened"). 0 deaths across 30 cynical players is itself a finding — see #3.

## Fixed THIS cycle — the P0 cluster (raised by 30/30 and ~25/30)

**1 + 2 + 4 — the NPC information-economy now works** (`src/modules/social/index.ts`, `content/hush/npcs.ts`):
- **The trade transacts with the natural verbs.** `give coins to Mox`, `pay Eun`, `bribe Eun`, `give the
  relic to Eun` all execute the same purchase. Before, only an undocumented bare `pay` worked and `give
  coins to X` dead-ended ("has no use for that").
- **The payment is actually spent** (the coin/relic is consumed; you can't buy the same map three times — a
  second purchase is refused), and **each merchant's prose is its own** (no more identical "creased law-map").
- **The purchase shows up in the codex.** The grant now sets `known.law.greywater: 'approximate'`, so the
  bought map is visible and honestly flagged "(from a bought map — unverified)". Before, buying set only
  `known.tell.*` and the codex's `if (stage === 'unknown') continue` skipped it → "the codex never updated".
- **Honest topic fall-through.** `ask <npc> about <unhandled>` now says "Nothing I can tell you about that"
  instead of silently replaying the greeting (which read as a parser miss and burned turns).
- **State-aware greetings.** Holt/Lyle/Mox/Eun now notice when you're carrying the core (a `when`-gated
  greeting) instead of greeting you identically as you walk out with it. Plus discovery topics (core/gap/price).
- Guarded by `test/hush/economy.test.ts` (5 cases). Gate green; golden unaffected (no trade in its tape).

## Backlog for next cycles (ranked, from the same 30 players)

- **P1 — certainty-decay has no teeth** (~17/30). Laws re-settle but no one suffers; hazards don't re-arm on
  backtrack. Make decay bite (re-arm a hazard) or cut it. The signature mechanic, currently wasted.
- **P1 — the "borrowed knife"/iron gap makes the Greywater inert** (~10/30). Intro says borrowed knife;
  inventory is lantern/coins (sometimes a crowbar). With no iron, the most-foreshadowed puzzle never forces
  the choice. Reconcile the opening with the starting kit. (Also explains the 0-deaths stakes problem.)
- **P1 — checkpoint HIDE untelegraphed** (~13/30). The solution only surfaces via a NOT_UNDERSTOOD error.
  Move the hint into the arrival description; surface `hide`. Caused 1 of the 2 non-wins.
- **P2 — `examine` returns the generic fallback on advertised props** (~8/30): the core itself, the Survey's
  index cards/law-tables, the crowbar. Author descriptions; make the crowbar say it's iron.
- **P2 — room descriptions repeat verbatim** across the three settlement rooms; don't update on revisit (~9/30).
- **P2 — Antenna Field is skippable dead content**; its law can't be deduced; the victory text over-claims
  "each law in turn" when you can win on two (~7/30).
- **P1-severity — the codex contradiction flag mis-times** (fires before two sources; persists after a law is
  deduced) (~4/30). Correctness bug in the central trust mechanic.

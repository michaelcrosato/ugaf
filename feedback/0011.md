# feedback/0011 — night4/night5: the teeth fixes VALIDATED, the muscle backlog confirmed

> Source: night4 (80 players) + night5 (100 players, with zone-specific rule-breakers). 180 blind players,
> all realness-VERIFIED. These two runs were FROZEN (no game edits during them), so the measurements are clean.

## What the swarm validated (the loop working, measured)

- **The Mile Road teeth (#11) are FAIR — unanimous among rule-breakers.** night4: all 8 rule-breakers died on
  the Mile Road, and every one called it fair: *"three escalating warnings, explicit terminal warning at the
  third, death on the fourth. I did not feel cheated. I felt like I'd earned it. That's good game design."*
  The death prose drew specific praise (*"'It simply stopped letting you arrive' — the right register"*). A
  swarm said "no teeth"; we added telegraphed teeth; the swarm verified them fair. The loop works.
- **The economy (#6/#10/#12) went from "the single worst thing" (night2) to "the best part of the game"
  (night3).** A measured worst→best swing from genuine blind critics.
- **The Antenna name-taboo is well-telegraphed (night5 antenna-breakers):** *"Pre-action warning: Excellent…
  a player who engaged with the space had full knowledge of the risk."* Calibrated: only deliberate repeated
  rule-breaking triggers a death; careful/rushing players win.
- **The Greywater cost is real, not a bluff (night5 greywater-breakers):** carrying iron through the night
  water degrades it, and that loss *bites downstream* — the ruined iron can no longer lever the wire-gap exit
  (*"it folds like warm wax… the Greywater ate its temper"*). An elegant, working consequence.

## The confirmed muscle backlog (engine-deep DESIGN work — do with fresh context)

night5 reconfirmed these as the top remaining, across personas:

1. **Economy DEPTH (the strongest recurring signal).** Mox's paid "safe hour / dry line" is *inert* — players
   *"never saw either mechanic in play"* and *"got the core without needing it… flavor text that cost me my
   entire economy."* Plus: paying spends the whole 3-coin roll for one law with *"no price posted in advance —
   a gotcha."* FIX DIRECTION: model coins as a count (price per law, a visible count); make a purchase grant
   something mechanically necessary you can't get free (a real safe-window the night Greywater enforces, or a
   route the deep water otherwise blocks); bind the purchase to the topic asked.
2. **Decay must bite (#3).** A re-Settle re-deduces to byte-identical text; *"a system with no teeth yet."*
   FIX DIRECTION: a re-Settle should flip which rumour is true (so a stale codex can now mislead you into a
   real cost), or be cut. This pairs with #1 — make the Greywater's night crossing genuinely threaten the run
   so protection (a fresh, non-stale law; a bought safe-window) is worth having.
3. Pacing: *"time advances on every action… a rail toward nightfall"* — thorough explorers hit night before
   they're ready (night5, multiple). Consider slowing the clock or making fewer actions cost game-time.
4. Navigation: distant zones (Greywater, Antenna) aren't shown as reachable from the checkpoint until you've
   walked the Mile Road — players *"couldn't plan a route."*

## Status

The lethal/telegraph/fairness systems are now sound and swarm-validated. The remaining work is design-heavy
(economy and decay depth) and was deferred deliberately rather than risk an unattended mistake. The harness,
the loop, and the validated fixes are the durable foundation; the muscle work is well-specified here for a
fresh-context build session.

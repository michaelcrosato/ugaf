## The verdict — the legibility/social fixes LANDED; the "no teeth" critique has collapsed onto ONE keystone

The night8 social + legibility work is **validated**. Deduce-legibility (#3) was reached by **19/24** and
named by name ("the best design decision in the game"; "good QA-of-itself — prevents getting stuck"). The
social design (#6 + #8) drew explicit praise from the **opus** tier: *"Lyle hedging bad lore, Eun refusing
to sell unverified laws and naming a free trustworthy source, Mox's topic-bound sales — genuinely good
systemic social design."* (systems-minmaxer, opus). The "NPC refusals are illegible" complaint correlated
with model size (it bit the haiku/sonnet players, not the opus tier) — **capability noise under §4.3**, not
the content axis (though the flat-`deflect` fallback string is still a real, minor coverage gap — see #6 below).

But the spine of the 0012 critique is unchanged, and **both cohorts + the strongest model are unanimous on
it**: the laws now bite everywhere *except the winning line*, and the winning line ends in a costless `hide`.
Behaviourally, across 22 usable players, the dominant win was **bought-map or basic — mastery wins were rare
(2 in cohort A, 0 in all-opus cohort B)**. The opus tier *bought* its way out 7/12 and hid for free. The
"learn the lethal laws" loop the whole demo teaches is, on the standard line, **optional**.

## Top game fixes (ranked by cross-persona breadth × cross-model survival)

### 1. The climax is a free win — `hide` is a skeleton key; the run's earned routes never get collected (P0)
- **Raised by 8+ personas across BOTH cohorts and EVERY tier** (soulslike-vet, narrative-critic,
  qa-breaker, systems-minmaxer, reckless-rusher, greywater-breaker, speedrunner; opus + sonnet) ·
  **confidence: maximum** (survives the §4.3 capability filter — unanimous in the all-opus cohort, not a
  weak-model artifact).
- In their words (all opus): *"Two doors were locked by my own earlier choices and the third opened on the
  first push… the most dangerous moment in The Hush is its safest."* (narrative-critic). *"Make the
  checkpoint a genuine branch: gate HIDE behind a real cost, make 'good iron' and 'Strider debt' reachable
  or hide them when prerequisites aren't met — don't list doors that won't open."* (qa-breaker). *"The iron
  consequence is your best-written mechanic and HIDE quietly nullifies it."* (greywater-breaker).
- **Behaviour check (§4.3, behaviour over self-report):** the iron rule is NOT toothless — greywater-iron
  tripped **18/24** and two players *watched the ore-knife close the wire-lever exit*. One opus player kept
  his knife two rooms back, pried the fence, and called it *"the one moment my earlier choices visibly paid
  off."* The design **already works** when `hide` isn't a free bypass. The bug is the free third route.
- **Fix (acceptance):** the checkpoint exit (carrying the core) must be a genuine, earned branch:
  - **PRY** shown only with working (non-ore) iron; if the Greywater dissolved it, the option is **gone and
    NAMED** ("your iron went soft in the bottoms — no good metal to lever the gap").
  - **DEBT** shown only with a Strider standing; otherwise signposted ("a Strider who owed you could walk you
    out — but you built no such debt").
  - **HIDE** must cost or risk something — gate it on cover of dark (it ties to the timing decision, #3
    below) and a calm patrol, so arriving *disarmed and unprepared* genuinely narrows your odds rather than
    being a guaranteed back door.
  - Acceptance: a player who arrives by day with dissolved iron and no debt is **not** handed a free escape;
    the climax shows only the routes currently open and *why* the others are closed.

### 2. Decay fires as an event but never lands a felt cost — AND can fire when you can no longer act (P1)
- **Raised by 5+ personas, both cohorts, opus-confirmed** (horror-skeptic, speedrunner, qa-breaker,
  greywater-breaker, soulslike-vet) · **confidence: high.**
- **Behaviour check:** decay-bit reached **15/24** — the mechanism (#5) demonstrably fires. So this is **not**
  "cut decay"; it is *"the predawn re-Settle never lands as a felt cost on the win line."* opus horror-skeptic:
  *"a law you trust but is now stale must be able to fail you at least once… until a player pays for stale
  knowledge even once, every '(certainty decaying)' is the game lying about its own stakes."* This is coupled
  to #1 — when `hide` stops being free, a stale law that cost you iron finally *matters* at the gate.
- **The unfair-death bug (qa-breaker's #1, both cohorts):** decay can fire for a law the player can no longer
  re-verify. *"A law should not expire while you are standing in the zone you'd need to re-enter to re-verify
  it… that is the one class of death this game absolutely cannot allow."* (Mile Road decayed at the Fork,
  core in hand.)
- **Fix (acceptance):** (a) **[higher priority — fairness]** a "certainty is decaying" warning must NOT fire
  for a law the player cannot safely re-verify; and a non-widening demote (Mile Road / Antenna) must reassure
  (the rule's *shape* still holds) rather than read as "you're guessing now." (b) the predawn bite must be
  reachable on a real line so stale knowledge charges at least once — folds into #1.

### 3. The core timed decision is made BLIND — the day→night threshold is invisible (P1)
- **Raised by the opus speedrunner (+ adjacent: impatient-normie, reckless-rusher)** · **confidence: medium-high**
  (single sharp opus voice, but it names the game's central mechanic).
- *"The entire game is built around one timed decision — cross the Greywater by day vs. drop iron vs. buy the
  hour — and right now the player makes it blind. Surface the day→night threshold: a turn counter to dusk, or
  escalating telegraphs ('the light's nearly gone'). And when you sell perishable knowledge like the safe
  hour, flag at the point of sale that it decays."*
- **Fix (acceptance):** escalating, concrete dusk-approach telegraphs before the phase flips (the status line
  shows the clock, but the *consequence* of the flip isn't legible); and Mox's safe-hour sale should note it
  is perishable.

### 4. The win-moment is unclear — after the escape, the room re-offers the escape and the road "silently un-blocks" (P1)
- **Raised by impatient-normie (opus) + greywater-breaker (both cohorts, the wire-gap geometry)** ·
  **confidence: medium** (likely a real flag/prose-update bug at the climax scene).
- *"After the decisive action the room offered the exact escape options I'd just executed… I had to guess
  that 'back to the waystation' (silently un-blocked) was the real finish. That confusion hits at the
  emotional peak."*
- **Fix (acceptance):** after the escape resolves (hide / pry / debt), the checkpoint prose must update to
  "the way is open" and the win transition should be unambiguous — don't re-list spent escape options.

### 5. The antenna second-name death collapses warning and death into one beat (P1/P2)
- **Raised by antenna-breaker in BOTH cohorts (behaviour-confirmed LOST both times)** · opus articulation:
  *"It contradicts the game's own deduced law ('heard a mile off — and the Changed COME toward it'). Insert a
  single beat — 'something turns toward your voice and begins to move' — that returns control so the player
  can flee, with death only if they speak again or stand still."*
- **Fix (acceptance):** the second spoken name should start the Changed *coming* (one actionable beat to flee
  the field), with death on the third name or on lingering — honouring the stated law and rewarding the
  player who learned it. Verify against the existing escape window (leaving the field already escapes after
  the first name) so the two don't double-count.

## The verdict — the fixes LANDED; the through-line has sharpened onto ONE root

The demo is genuinely good, and the cynics agree: all 26 — including the harshest opus critics — praised
the cold open, the prose, the parser's robustness, and the **fairness** of the fatal rules. The night9
batch is validated. But the spine of the critique is now unanimous, behaviour-confirmed, and **narrower
than ever**: the atmosphere and writing write checks the mechanics never cash — **on the winning path, no
law ever bites.**

Behaviour proves it isn't grumbling. Across 26 realness-verified players: **WON·mastery = 0** (both tiers —
the all-opus capability cohort took zero mastery wins too, so this is real content signal, not weak-model
noise), 13 bought-map wins, 9 basic wins, **zero timed out**, and the only four deaths were the two
rule-breakers and two antenna-breakers who *deliberately* walked into a fatal rule to test it. The antenna
field — the richest, most-praised hazard, now fair — was reached by **zero winners on the way out**. The
Hollow Dark linger-trap fired **0/26** even when players report lingering on purpose.

This is the same "needs teeth" complaint, collapsed from "the climax" (0013) to its true root: **a clean
line to the waystation bench never has to put one lethal law to the test.** The next batch's keystone is to
**route the critical path through at least one failable law** — and the antenna field, already built and
already fair, is the obvious candidate.

## Top game fixes (ranked by cross-persona breadth × cross-model survival)

### 1. The winning path bypasses every lethal law — make one failable on the critical path (P1, keystone)
- **Raised by 9 personas** (soulslike-vet, parser-purist, speedrunner, narrative-critic, qa-breaker,
  systems-minmaxer, reckless-rusher, greywater-breaker, impatient-normie) · **sonnet + opus** · **confidence
  high** (the root the next several fixes are facets of).
- *"I beat the Hush without ever putting one lethal law to the test"* (b-p000, opus); *"the detective
  mechanic is a quiz with no failing grade"* (a-p003, opus). Behaviour: the antenna field was reached by
  only 6/26 and by **zero winners on the way out**.
- **Fix (acceptance):** route core-retrieval *or* the only viable exit through the antenna field (it is
  built and its death is already fair). A clean speed-line must not reach the waystation bench without
  committing to one deduced law at a choke-point where the wrong call is survivable-but-costly or fatal.

### 2. Paid knowledge is strictly dominated and produces no agency (P1)
- **Raised by 10 personas** (speedrunner, systems-minmaxer, reckless-rusher, rule-breaker, soulslike-vet,
  qa-breaker, impatient-normie, horror-skeptic, greywater-breaker, narrative-critic) · **sonnet + opus** ·
  **confidence high** (the single most-cited concrete grievance).
- *"Paying Mox is strictly dominated by 'just leave your iron at the fork' — but the game never tells you
  that's an option, so players burn a coin on nothing"* (a-p002, sonnet). And b-p007 (opus): *"I paid for
  the safe hour, was told it drifted, and then had no way to **act** on that"* — the purchase never becomes a
  command.
- **Fix (acceptance):** (a) give the safe-hour purchase an actionable verb surface in the field (`wait for
  the safe hour` / `cross now`) where using it preserves iron and ignoring it costs something later needed;
  (b) stop the free "drop iron" line from strictly dominating (e.g. the core itself is worked metal you must
  protect across the ford). A buy-and-use player reaches an outcome a no-shop player can't trivially
  replicate by setting down one item.

### 3. Over-telegraphing: the dusk warning and the three rules nag 4–5× nearly verbatim (P2)
- **Raised by 9 personas** (qa-breaker, systems-minmaxer, soulslike-vet, parser-purist, narrative-critic,
  impatient-normie, horror-skeptic, greywater-breaker, antenna-breaker) · **sonnet + opus** · **confidence
  high.** This is the night9 #3 telegraph *over-corrected* — it lands universally (26/26) but repeats.
- *"One good warning lands. Five is the game elbowing me in the ribs"* (b-p004, opus). The same
  iron/look-back/name rule is restated by Notices → Holt → Lyle → Eun → codex.
- **Fix (acceptance):** collapse the dusk transition to one strong beat at the dusk→night flip plus at most
  one reminder; de-duplicate the three rules so each source adds *new* information. In a normal run the dusk
  warning fires ≤2 times and no single rule is stated identically by more than two sources. (Keep the
  coverage — just cut the echo.)

### 4. The certainty-decay / law-drift system announces itself and never has a consequence (P2)
- **Raised by 8 personas** (soulslike-vet, qa-breaker, speedrunner, narrative-critic, systems-minmaxer,
  impatient-normie, greywater-breaker, parser-purist) · **sonnet + opus** · **confidence high.**
- *"Law-drift + certainty-decay is the single most interesting idea here. It had **zero** mechanical
  consequence on my run"* (b-p006, opus). Behaviour: `decay-bit(predawn)` reached by 10, `saw-decay-warning`
  by 10 — exercised by ten players, **all** report no felt effect. b-p001 (opus) flags a perverse case: a
  null `examine` that returned nothing *still* fired "your certainty is decaying" — punishing looking, in a
  game about looking.
- **Fix (acceptance):** either (a) make stale knowledge mislead — a decayed law gives a fuzzy/wrong reading
  you must re-deduce before it's safe to act on — or (b) cut the decay text. And stop decay firing on empty
  examines. At least one run-state where acting on decayed knowledge yields a worse outcome than acting on
  fresh; null examines do not decay certainty. (Couples to #1: a stale law that costs you at the failable
  choke-point is where decay finally bites.)

### 5. The extraction climax is a gimme — HIDE is handed over and the manhunt never materializes (P2)
- **Raised by 8 personas** (soulslike-vet, parser-purist, qa-breaker, narrative-critic, systems-minmaxer,
  impatient-normie, horror-skeptic, reckless-rusher) · **sonnet + opus** · **confidence high.** (0013 #1
  gave the gate teeth; the swarm now finds the *standard* line still strolls — the literal `(HIDE)` prompt
  and an auto-succeeding hide.)
- *"The climax is a cutscene that hands you the win"* (b-p003, opus); *"the game capitalized the verb **for**
  me and there was no visible risk"* (a-p001, opus).
- **Fix (acceptance):** (a) remove the literal `(HIDE)` from the checkpoint room text — describe the dark
  wedge and crooked floodlight, accept `hide`/`sneak`/`slip`/`go low`/`creep`; (b) give the walk-out one
  losable check that *uses* a learned law (a blowable stealth beat, or the Striders actually moving on the
  core so the player must spend iron / a debt / nerve). The extraction has ≥1 losable check and `(HIDE)` no
  longer appears verbatim in room text.

### 6. The Hollow Dark linger-death never fires (0/26) and the `nerve` stat it governs is never shown (P1)
- **Raised by 5 personas** (systems-minmaxer, greywater-breaker, qa-breaker, speedrunner, soulslike-vet) ·
  **sonnet + opus** · **confidence high** (behaviour-confirmed; reads as broken/unreachable, not merely
  shallow).
- *"I examine'd, search'd, and wait'd repeatedly in the Greywater Bottoms... Nothing ever happened. A threat
  you state but never enforce is worse than no threat"* (b-p006, opus). Behaviour: `hollow-dark = 0` despite
  deliberate lingering. b-p004 (opus): *"`nerve`... is named in the law but invisible on the HUD."*
- **Fix (acceptance):** verify the linger trigger actually fires; set its threshold so a deliberate waiter
  reaches it within a run; surface `nerve` on the status line. A player who waits N turns in the deep after
  dark sees `nerve` drop and eventually dies, with `nerve` visible throughout. (Note: the Greywater Bottoms
  is *outside* the Hollow Dark's scope — that may be the unreachability; verify scope vs. the deep nodes.)

### 7. A stealth-gated exit returns `[NOT_UNDERSTOOD]`, teaching players to distrust valid commands (P2)
- **Raised by 4 personas** (qa-breaker, horror-skeptic, reckless-rusher, antenna-breaker) · **sonnet +
  opus** · **confidence high.**
- *"Tagging a meaningful, correct response as NOT_UNDERSTOOD teaches the player to distrust their own valid
  commands"* (b-p010, opus). The wire-gap command is *understood* but gated on being hidden; the game
  mislabels the gate as a parse failure.
- **Fix (acceptance):** return a diegetic refusal ("The floodlight sweeps the open ground — you'd be seen.
  Get low first.") instead of `[NOT_UNDERSTOOD]`. The gated exit never returns a parse-failure tag for a
  recognized command.

### 8. `deduce` over-coaches capable players (P2 — dial back, do NOT remove)
- **Raised by 5 personas** (narrative-critic, systems-minmaxer, soulslike-vet, speedrunner,
  greywater-breaker) · **sonnet + opus** · **confidence high** · *caveat: the mechanic itself is widely
  praised (see What's Working) and the hint helps weaker players.*
- *"The system's only function is to tax players for not already knowing what the game just told them"*
  (b-p002, opus) — points at a genuine redundancy (rules given free on the notices, then re-derived for a
  time cost).
- **Fix (acceptance):** show the "try: deduce X" pointer and live sign-count once, then stop; ensure
  deduction unlocks something the free notices do *not* (the actionable safe-hour, a mitigation), so
  "knowing" beats "nerve". Deducing a law grants a concrete mechanical advantage unavailable from reading
  the notices. (Dovetails with #2 and #4.)

## Parser-confound (verify, do not rank as content)
- **`buy`/`purchase` not aliased to the trade action → `[NOT_UNDERSTOOD]`** — 4 personas (rule-breaker,
  parser-purist, horror-skeptic, greywater-breaker). The NPC literally says the way in is *"for sale,"* yet
  `buy the safe hour from Mox` bounces; only `give coins`/`pay` work. Almost certainly a real verb-coverage
  gap — alias `buy`/`purchase` → trade and verify.
- **Verbose travel strings** ("the maintained road, down to Lyle's Rest") work when copied but paraphrases
  ("go to fork", "fork") may bounce — a-p002, a-p004, a-p009 flag as latent. Verify short aliases resolve.
- **Narrow item verbs** — `drop knife` works but `leave`/`stow knife` untested (a-p004). Verify synonyms.
- **Noun-scoping at the ford** — `examine blade`/`examine knife` describes the *environmental* salvager's
  blade, not the player's inventory `knife (ore)` (b-p004, b-p011, b-p012). A scoping issue at a dramatic
  beat — verify which noun wins scope; it can masquerade as "I couldn't inspect my loss."

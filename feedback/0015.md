## The verdict — the keystone LANDED the difficulty; it had not yet landed the legibility

The keystone did exactly what feedback/0014 demanded: it put a deduced, failable law on the win path. The
strolls the prior swarm condemned are dead — **the win rate fell from night9's ~85% to 1/13**, and the
behaviour confirms the laws now bite on the line (`greywater-iron`=11/13; **core-lost-to-greywater = 10/13,
every one of them WARNED first** by the rung-1/2 ladder). The single winner took the patient, deduce-then-time
line (73 turns, core intact). So the mechanic is *sound and fair-once-understood*.

But it was **not learnable**, and that is a §4.5 tell-sufficiency failure the gate + evaluator structurally
cannot catch — only blind play can. The world teaches "the Greywater eats **iron**"; the core is pointedly
*not* iron, so players reasonably dropped their iron and carried the non-iron core out to its loss — a rule
that *contradicted what they were taught*, revealed only at the bite. soulslike-vet (the brutal-but-fair
FromSoftware lens) nailed it: *"Every warning said iron dies in the water at night… Nothing said anomalous
matter was also vulnerable… a rules expansion that arrived at the worst possible moment."* And the
uncertain-intent guard leaked a raw engine string (`[NOT_UNDERSTOOD] K8 … core_lost`) at that same beat,
shredding trust. **This is the loop working exactly as the blueprint intends:** ship a hypothesis →
blind-validate → the un-oracle-able fairness gap surfaces → fix.

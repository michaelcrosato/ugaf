## The frontier moved — from "the win is unfair" to "the plumbing leaks"

Now that the earned win is fair and reachable, the critique sharpened onto concrete defects (ranked):

### 1. The time economy forces dead-time wait-spam, and `wait until <phase>` is a LIE (P1) — a side-effect of the keystone
- **5 personas (soulslike-vet, parser-purist, speedrunner, narrative-critic, qa-breaker), opus + sonnet.**
- The keystone made players wait out the dark to cross the Greywater safely — which exposed that the
  fast-forward verb doesn't fast-forward: *"`wait until dawn` advances thirty minutes"* (p003); *"a loading
  screen with extra steps"* (p000). Players typed `wait` ~30× to pass one night.
- **Fix (acceptance):** `wait until <dawn|midday|dusk|night>` / `sleep` / `rest` at a SAFE location jumps to
  that phase boundary in ONE turn (`wait until dawn` at 21:00 → ~06:14, not +30 min). Add a "skip to the next
  safe crossing window" action at the ford / Lyle's Rest. This is the highest-leverage night11 item — it is
  the keystone's own dead-time, and it hits even the winners. (Remove the dwell; do NOT fill it with bait.)

### 2. The Greywater deduction never resolves — "2 of 2 signs" yet "go deeper" (P1, a real bug)
- **3 personas (soulslike-vet, qa-breaker, systems-minmaxer), sonnet** (the two opus winners bought the map
  and bypassed the deduction — coverage, not contradiction).
- *"The game says '2 of 2 signs found' and simultaneously says 'go deeper'… a bug in the feedback loop of the
  core mechanic"* (p000). p012 localizes it: *"breaks AFTER the law re-Settled."* A **mastery win was reached
  with this law unverified** (p004) — so confirm the win-gate doesn't depend on the broken counter.
- **Fix:** when `signs_read == signs_required`, `deduce the greywater` resolves to `certain` and writes the
  verified law. Investigate the drift/re-Settle path first (counter not reset on drift, or a stale resolve flag).

### 3. The Antenna Field is half-wired: relic trade misroutes (bug) + the pillar is off the win path (P1/P2)
- **5 personas, opus + sonnet.** Part A (bug): `give relic to Eun` routes to **Mox's** coin handler — *"the
  'you already carry that map' response"* fires out of context. **Likely the night9 #6 `shop-pointer-deflect`
  handler is swallowing the give-verb at the Survey** — a regression to investigate. Part B (design): the win
  route doesn't pass the antennas, so a third of the advertised rule-system is decorative. **Fix:** route the
  relic give to Eun's antenna-glass trade (or an honest refusal naming what she wants — never Mox's string);
  and either thread a win-relevant branch through the Antenna Field or trim its dialogue weight to match its
  optional status (the mechanic is sound — p010 confirms; the problem is reachability).

### 4. Advertised stakes that never fire: Hollow Dark (0/13) and certainty-decay read as bluffs (P2)
- **5 personas.** `hollow-dark` tripped **0/13**; `decay-bit` fired for 7 but none felt a consequence. *"I
  waited five times in the flooded pump-house at 3 AM… nothing… it teaches the player the rule is a bluff"*
  (p006 — the pump-house is a safe pocket that isn't communicated). **Fix:** make the pump-house's
  safe-pocket status explicit, or clarify the Hollow Dark trigger so the *open* deep after dark costs a
  legible nerve; give decay one decision-bearing payoff (a stale law misleads you at a crossing) or cut the
  nag. Do NOT add re-deduction grind (Goodhart).

### 5. Mox's transaction is opaque — the safe-hour window isn't stated at purchase (P2)
- **4 personas, sonnet + haiku** (learnability exemption — the haiku signal stands). The keystone made the
  bought safe-hour load-bearing, but *"the safe hour's deadline is invisible"* (p005). **Fix:** at purchase,
  Mox states the window ("the bottoms sleep at midday — cross *then*") and frames it as luck bought, not
  learned. (The win-screen already lands the "bought luck" theme — move part of it to purchase time.)

### 6. Prose over-explains its ending + repeats (P2, DOWN-WEIGHTED)
- 6 personas by count, but down-weighted: "show-don't-tell / overused phrase" is exactly the critique LLM
  reviewers are pretrained to volunteer, and p012 found the same ending *"lands without overreaching."* Light
  polish: trust the player, cut the closing aphorism; "held breath" recurs 3–4×.

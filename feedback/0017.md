## The frontier moved: from "is the win earned?" (yes) to "do the rules ever BITE the player who learns them?"

Now that careful play reliably wins, the sharpest critics — *the ones who won* — converge on a single,
higher-order critique, and it is exactly the standing top priority (render real consequences for world
dangers; make them bite harder).

### 1. The rules punish disobedience but create no DILEMMAS — knowing them removes all tension (P1, the keystone)
- **systems-minmaxer (sonnet, WON), corroborated by narrative-critic (opus, WON) and soulslike-vet/p000 (sonnet, WON).** Spans the size axis → CONTENT signal, not capability noise.
- The systems-minmaxer, the ideal judge, says it plainest: *"The rules never bit. … The setup promises
  adversity under rules. The execution delivers a guided tour where knowing the rules is sufficient to
  feel nothing."* Per-law:
  - **Mile Road:** *"a corpse in a thousand-loop death-spiral of bootprints. Excellent staging. I walked
    forward and nothing tested me. Zero temptation to look back was ever created."*
  - **Antenna Field:** *"I walked through in silence and nothing happened. There was no NPC to call out
    to, no situation where speaking was even tempting."*
  - **Greywater:** *"showed up in the morning … trivially managed. I never once felt the water waking up."*
- The mechanism: each law punishes a *mistake* (look back / speak a name / carry iron at night), so a
  player who has learned the law simply never makes the mistake, and sails through with **zero felt
  tension**. The dangers are a *checklist*, not a *gauntlet*. The corroborating decay finding is the
  same shape — narrative-critic: *"the law-'drift' decay nags … but never paid off in my run — pure
  anxiety tax."*
- **Fix (acceptance) — design the dangers to create DILEMMAS, not avoidance-by-knowing.** The
  showcase-slice bar ("10 minutes to finish, hours to keep playing") needs the laws to *force
  engagement*, e.g.: a reason you might *have* to look back or move under threat (a pursuit beat); a
  situation where staying silent at the antennas has a real cost (a sound you must make, or an NPC/lure
  that tempts a name); a Greywater window tight enough that the timing is a genuine gamble, not a
  morning stroll. **Goodhart guard (§4.3):** this is *not* "add dwell/grind" and *not* re-deduction
  grind — it is converting "don't do X" into "weigh X against Y." Engagement is never the maximand;
  *tension under a learnable rule* is. This is the night13 keystone.

### 2. The endgame's tension dissolves, and the exit reads as a soft-lock (P1)
- **narrative-critic (opus), systems-minmaxer (sonnet), soulslike-vet/p000 (sonnet).** 3 independent
  personas across tiers → high confidence.
- Two faces of one problem — the carefully-built endgame pressure ("the Cordon will be watching the
  gate") evaporates:
  - **The bought exit auto-resolves with no agency.** systems-minmaxer: *"Mox's prior payment clears
    the checkpoint immediately. The tension dissolved with zero friction … I'd accidentally purchased
    an exit-skip."* p000: *"The Strider steps up … I didn't *do* anything."*
  - **The exit text lies / hides the real route (B10).** narrative-critic, carrying a ruined knife, was
    told *"that road out is closed, and the Greywater closed it,"* trekked eight rooms back to the ford
    to retrieve the knife (because the ford said *"in daylight … the metal is just metal"*), found it
    still `ore`, and *"nearly quit believing I'd soft-locked myself"* — while the working exit (`HIDE`,
    revealed only at dusk) *"sat one nightfall away, undisclosed."* Two contradictions: the ford prose
    implies daylight un-rusts a slumped blade (it doesn't — the slump is permanent), and the "closed"
    text never surfaces the *other* open routes.
- **Fix:** the bought exit should require an *act* (lean on the debt / ask Mox to walk you out), not
  fire passively; the "closed" exit text must name the routes that ARE open (HIDE after dark, the
  Strider's debt) instead of dead-ending; and the ford prose must not imply a permanently-slumped knife
  recovers by daylight.

### 3. The fork/ford homophone + a clarify-loop can cost the timed core escape (P1) — see B7
- **speedrunner (sonnet, LOST to it), corroborated by qa-breaker/p003's geography note.** "the fork"
  (the safe junction, out of the water) and "the Greywater **ford**" (the hungry water) are
  near-homophones, and the escape from the bottoms is bottoms→ford(still water)→fork(safe). A
  time-pressured player says *"back to the ford"* (still hungry water), the K8 irreversible-confirm
  fires on the core slump and **loops** (*"say your move again"* cannot raise confidence on an
  unresolvable noun), and the 1–2-move escape margin is lost. Intricate — touches a node rename (IDs,
  exits, many tests) and/or the safety-critical K8 path. **Deferred for careful design + night12c
  corroboration; not rushed at the game's most safety-critical beat.** Candidate fixes in the tracker.

### 4. P2s — verified, ranked, down-weighted
- **The relic is redundant once you've bought the Greywater table** (systems-minmaxer, p000). night12's
  #3B signpost makes it *discoverable* (a coin-free path), but a player who paid Mox/Eun finds it does
  nothing new. It needs a *distinct* value, not a substitute for the table. P2.
- **Decay is an anxiety-tax with no in-run payoff** (p000, narrative-critic). Confirmed: the drift
  warning fires but the predawn bite is rarely reached in a single session (it pauses during the core
  carry-out; the payoff is long-horizon — §4.5 says blind agents cannot measure it). **Soften the nag**
  (don't alarm about a consequence a single-session player won't meet) rather than add grind. P2.
- **NPCs are static lore-vendors** (narrative-critic): Mox recites the Greywater law verbatim even after
  you've deduced it. Dialogue should notice what you already know. P2.
- **Minor parser:** `sell relic to Mox` bounced where `give` worked (p000) — add a `sell`/`trade` alias.

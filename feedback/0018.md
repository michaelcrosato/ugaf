## The next mountain (P1 keystone, cross-tier + cross-persona + multi-cohort): THE ENDGAME IS HOLLOW

The game promises a climax — *"Word of the core moves faster than you can. By the time you turn for
home, the Cordon will be watching the gate"* — and then the threat never arrives. The winners, the
players best equipped to judge it, converge:

- **soulslike-vet (opus):** *"Make the Striders actually fire. The 'you still have to walk out past all
  of them' buildup is the climax and it is hollow… A brutal-but-fair game lives or dies on whether the
  danger it promises arrives."*
- **systems-minmaxer (opus):** *"The `hide` exit must not be a free universal escape hatch. Gate it on
  something the destroyed knife can't cover — a patrol-rousing check, a distraction you have to
  engineer (this is where the antenna name-trick or a coin bribe should finally matter), a timing
  window. Then losing the metal lever genuinely matters."*
- **p000 (soulslike-vet, sonnet):** *"The checkpoint exit resolved itself ('The Strider steps up') — I
  didn't *do* anything."*

**Root cause (code-confirmed):** the SLIP gate is hollow — `flag.hidden` is set permanently by one
`hide` and *never cleared*, and patrol `awareness` only rises on a *loud* act a carry-out never
performs, so HIDE is a no-op check; and the Strider debt opens the gate *passively* (a bare fact test,
no act). So none of the earned choices — keep vs drop iron, pay Mox, learn the gap, brave the
antennas — **diverge** at the exit.

**This unifies "the rules never bit."** Same disease: promised danger doesn't arrive for the careful
player. The exit is the highest-leverage place to cure it.

**Fix (acceptance) — make the exit a gated, divergent challenge** (designed; first swing): the Strider
debt must require an *act* (lean on it), and HIDE must beat a *live* patrol-watch (carrying working
iron *clinks* and re-rouses it). Each of the three routes still wins (LEARN: travel quiet / drop iron →
silent HIDE; BUY: lean on the debt; STRIP/TIME: pry the gate with iron), but now they **diverge** and
the climax has teeth — and the antenna name-trick gets a real job (a distraction that finally matters).
Fairness sacred: telegraphed, recoverable, never an untelegraphed block; all three routes stay viable.

## #2 — the time economy is a recurring pain (3+ personas)
- **The fast-forward exists but isn't discovered.** `wait until <phase>` (a real single-turn skip,
  shipped night11) was used by *nobody* who needed it — systems-minmaxer typed plain `wait` *"fourteen
  identical ticks."* **Fix:** hint the fast-forward when a player repeats plain `wait` at a safe node
  (or auto-fast-forward a plain `wait` there). The mechanic is the game's best (per the minmaxer); it
  just hides.
- **The intel time-tax (edge case).** examine/ask cost ~10 min each, so a *thorough* player can burn
  the daylight and be forced into a doomed night carry-out (parser-purist night12a, soulslike-vet
  night13a — the one careful loss). Careful players usually win, so this is legibility, not systemic —
  but it interacts with the keystone: the time budget *should* be the legible strategic resource that
  makes the laws bite.

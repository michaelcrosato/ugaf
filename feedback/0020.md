## The frontier moved: the laws are OVER-said, but the Greywater CLOCK is UNDER-said (the dominant loss mode)

Ranked by cross-persona breadth, capability-filtered (learnability stalls exempt from the size-axis discard):

### 1. The crossing deadline is under-telegraphed while the rules are over-telegraphed (P1, the dominant loss mode)
- **6 personas, all three tiers** (parser-purist [opus], speedrunner/qa-breaker/rule-breaker [sonnet],
  impatient-normie/horror-skeptic [haiku]). **5 of 7 losses** = carried the core through the Greywater after
  dark → it dissolved. The behaviour proves the window is *navigable* (`mox-window`=4, all four buyers won;
  `dusk-telegraph` fired 12/12) — so the fix is not "make it possible," it is "make the timing legible early
  and free."
- The two complaints resolve into one: **the rules are over-recited; the timing is under-said.**
  qa-breaker (LOST): *"The game pushes you to learn before you go, then punishes you for having done so.
  That's a design contradiction, not a difficulty curve."* impatient-normie (LOST): *"you're punishing the
  player for not meta-gaming the clock — that's not a fair puzzle, that's a trap."* And the mirror:
  parser-purist: *"the same three rules recited by six sources with no danger… by the fifth rephrasing I was
  skimming."*
- **Fix (acceptance):** before the player commits to the deep (the opening notices or Holt, *free*), state
  (a) the concrete hour by which the Greywater must be crossed, and (b) that the **core**, not just iron,
  dissolves after dark. Cut two of the redundant rule-recitations to pay for it. **Learnability exception
  (§4.3):** haiku ate this hardest, opus adapted — but this is content signal, not capability noise; the fix
  raises the floor without removing the challenge the strong players called *fair*. **The next keystone (night17).**

### 2. The core's night-water decay is a 2-move solve with no meter (P1)
- **3 personas (greywater-breaker, impatient-normie, horror-skeptic; sonnet + haiku).** The *recovery* half of
  #1. greywater-breaker is surgical: *"pick up core → 1 move → 'last margin' → 1 more move → core is ore →
  game over. A puzzle with a 2-move solve window and no meter."* horror-skeptic: *"I needed to know the core
  was degrading, not have it revealed as outcome."*
- **Fix (acceptance):** extend the night-water countdown from 2 moves to ~4, and render inventory state as
  `core (degrading)` → `core (failing)` → `core (ore)` so a moving player can recover and *sees* the state.
  Keep the rule fatal — both players affirm the *rule* is right; only the window and the feedback are unfair.
  (Pairs with #1 as night17; this one needs a golden re-pin — the canonical path crosses the Greywater.)

### 4. The Hollow Dark threat text fires out of context (daylight / safe shelters) — a learnability bug (P1)

## The tool-caught keystone (this is the real frontier for #1) — the Hollow Dark under-bites for TWO structural reasons, not the shelter
A deterministic tool sweep + the dawdler persona (opus, ~30 wait commands, the law LIVE in its seed)
established **why** the swarm keeps reporting "the Hollow Dark never bites":

1. **LIVENESS (the loop confound):** `liveLaws: { min: 3, max: 4, always: [mile_road, greywater,
   antenna_field] }` puts the Hollow Dark in the rotating pool — measured **live in only 17/40 seeds
   (43%)**. In the other ~57% the law is simply OFF; it cannot bite, and the digest's "trips / all runs"
   conflates that with toothlessness.
2. **NIGHT-ONLY FIRING (the dominant constraint, behaviour-confirmed):** the law's `ambientGate` is
   `night`, so a dawdler cycling through day/night barely accumulates. The `dawdler` (the law LIVE):
   *"across ~30 wait commands it charged my danger counters exactly once, to unsettled 1 / exposure 1, and
   then never presented its bill before the run ended."* Even a dedicated lingerer, with the law on, was
   charged **once** — because sustained *night* stillness at a scoped node almost never happens (waits and
   fast-forwards roll the clock through daylight, where the law sleeps).

**Conclusion:** the finite-shelter grace (night23) correctly closes the *infinite-shelter* hole and is fair,
but it is **low-impact** — the binding constraints on "idling never bites" are liveness + night-timing, which
it does not touch. Ship it as a correct fix; the real #1 fix is below.
